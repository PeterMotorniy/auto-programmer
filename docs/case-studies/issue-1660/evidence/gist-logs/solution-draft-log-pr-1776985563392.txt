### Error Explanation

The fatal error in the 2006 Singh Anand proof is that the author confuses **provability within PA** with **truth only in the standard model**.

Specifically:
1. The author proves `(∀x)G(x)` in PA by induction (where `G(x) := x = 0 ∨ ∃y, x = S(y)`)
2. He incorrectly concludes this eliminates non-standard models of PA

**Why it fails**: By Gödel's Completeness Theorem, if `(∀x)G(x)` is provable in PA, it holds in ALL models—including non-standard ones. In non-standard models, each non-standard element ω satisfies `G(ω)` because ω = S(ω-1) (it is a successor of another non-standard element). The formula does not exclude non-standard models; it holds in them.

This is directly refuted by Gödel's Incompleteness Theorem + Löwenheim-Skolem Theorem, which together guarantee that first-order PA has non-standard models.

### Formalization Details

- **Proof files**: Formalize Anand's argument step by step, marking the invalid inference with `sorry`/`Admitted`
- **Refutation files**: Prove that `G(x)` holds in non-standard models (via Gödel's Completeness Theorem), and show that Anand's claim leads to a direct contradiction with known model-theory results
